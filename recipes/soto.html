Soto Pak Agus BK

Soto is a traditional Indonesian soup known for its rich, aromatic broth and hearty ingredients. To prepare Soto Ayam (chicken soto), start by making the broth: boil 1 liter of water with 500 grams of chicken, 2 smashed lemongrass stalks, 4 kaffir lime leaves, and 2 bay leaves. Meanwhile, blend a spice paste using 4 cloves of garlic, 5 shallots, 2 candlenuts, 1 teaspoon of turmeric, 1 teaspoon of coriander seeds, and a small piece of galangal. Saut√© the paste in a bit of oil until fragrant, then add to the boiling broth. Let it simmer for 30 minutes, allowing the chicken to cook through and absorb the spices. Remove the chicken, shred it, and set aside. Strain the broth to remove any solids. To serve, place the shredded chicken in a bowl, pour the hot broth over it, and add toppings such as boiled eggs, bean sprouts, sliced cabbage, and fried shallots. Garnish with fresh lime wedges, chopped cilantro, and a drizzle of sambal for a spicy touch. Serve with steamed rice or vermicelli noodles for a comforting and aromatic meal.

Bakso Recipes

  • Meatballs (Bakso Balls):
    • Ground beef (or a mixture of beef and pork)
    • Tapioca starch (or cornstarch)
    • Salt and pepper
    • Garlic, finely minced
    • Baking powder (optional, for texture)
  • Soup (Kuah Bakso):
    • Beef broth (can be homemade or store-bought)
    • Garlic, minced
    • Shallots, minced
    • Ginger, sliced or minced
    • Indonesian sweet soy sauce (kecap manis)
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • Chinese celery or regular celery, chopped (optional)
  • Additional Ingredients (Optional):
    • Sliced beef (for additional meatiness)
    • Carrots, thinly sliced
    • Cabbage, shredded
    • Bok choy or choy sum
    • Fried shallots (for garnish)
    • Sambal (chili paste) or fresh chili (for spice)
  • Condiments (Served on the side):
    • Indonesian soy sauce (kecap manis)
    • Sambal (chili paste)
    • Lime wedges
  • Noodles (if serving with noodles):
    • Egg noodles (cooked separately according to package instructions)

How to Make Bakso

  1. Prepare the Meatballs:
    1. Mix ground beef (or beef and pork mixture) with tapioca starch, salt, pepper, minced garlic, and optional baking powder until well combined.
    2. Form small meatballs using your hands or a small scoop. Set aside.
  2. Prepare the Soup:
    1. In a large pot, heat beef broth over medium heat.
    2. Add minced garlic, shallots, and sliced or minced ginger. Cook until fragrant.
    3. Season with Indonesian sweet soy sauce (kecap manis), salt, and pepper to taste.
    4. Add Chinese celery or regular celery (optional) for additional flavor.
  3. Cook the Meatballs:
    1. Gently drop the meatballs into the simmering broth.
    2. Cook until the meatballs float to the surface and are cooked through, about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Add Additional Ingredients:
    1. If desired, add sliced beef, thinly sliced carrots, shredded cabbage, and bok choy or choy sum to the soup. Cook until vegetables are tender.
  5. Assemble and Serve:
    1. If serving with noodles, cook egg noodles separately according to package instructions.
    2. Place cooked noodles (if using) in serving bowls.
    3. Ladle hot soup with meatballs and vegetables over the noodles.
    4. Garnish with fried shallots and serve with Indonesian soy sauce (kecap manis), sambal (chili paste), and lime wedges on the side.
